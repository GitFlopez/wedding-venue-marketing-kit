## 1. HERO VENUE DESCRIPTION (150 words)

Imagine your ceremony framed by the Spring Mountains — live olive trees lining the aisle, warm desert light catching every detail as your guests settle into the outdoor garden. At Desert Rose Estate, those mountains don't just sit in the background. They're your backdrop, your witness, and the reason every photograph looks like it belongs in a magazine.

Inside the Edison-lit reception hall, modern rustic details meet desert-chic warmth: polished concrete, warm wood, and the soft glow of hundreds of string lights overhead. Our covered heated patio bridges the two worlds — perfect for cocktail hour, late-night dancing, or whenever Henderson evenings turn cool.

From intimate elopements of two to Grand Affairs of 300, every package includes in-house catering, a full open bar, and the steady hand of a day-of coordinator who has done this more times than she can count.

## 2. BRAND STORY (200 words)

I came to wedding coordination the long way.

I spent a decade in hospitality management before I realized that the events I cared most about — the ones I stayed late for, the ones I replayed on the drive home — were always the weddings. So I got my CWEP, found this property at the edge of Henderson with its sweep of mountain views, and built Desert Rose Estate around a single belief: your wedding should feel like yours, not like the venue's.

What that looks like in practice: we don't double-book. Every weekend, one couple. That means your flowers stay exactly where you put them, your catering team knows your aunt's allergy, and the sound system is tuned for your first dance — not whoever was here the night before.

Our in-house team handles catering, the bar (licensed under Nevada Catering Liquor Permit #NV-CLP-20241184), and day-of coordination. Our preferred vendor list of 14 local professionals handles everything else. You bring the vision; we make it happen.

If you've been searching for a wedding venue that treats your day as the only day that matters — this is the one.

— Priya Vasquez, CWEP
Founder, Desert Rose Estate
