## 4. OBJECTION HANDLER SCRIPTS (Word-for-Word)

### A. "We're still looking at other venues"

"That makes complete sense — this is one of the biggest decisions of your planning process, and you should feel completely certain before you sign anything. What I can tell you is that Desert Rose Estate has one thing most venues in this area don't: we host one wedding per weekend. That means your flowers stay exactly where you arranged them, your linens are set for your event, and on your wedding morning the only team on this property is yours. If you're comparing us side-by-side with another venue, I'm happy to walk through exactly what's included here so you have an accurate comparison. What would be most helpful?"

---

### B. "Your price is higher than [Competitor]"

"I appreciate you being upfront — it helps me give you a straight answer. The most important thing to compare is what's actually included in each number. Our Grand Affair package includes in-house catering, a full licensed open bar, day-of coordination, and a bridal suite. When you price those separately at most venues, you're typically looking at $14,000-19,000 before you've paid the venue rental. Our package covers all of it in one number, with one team, and one invoice. If you can share what the other venue's all-in number looks like with the same inclusions, I'd love to walk through it with you — I think the comparison will surprise you."

---

### C. "We need to think about it / talk to family"

"Of course — take all the time you need. I do want to be transparent with you: your [DATE] date is one of three remaining Saturdays I have open this summer, and once it's released, I can't guarantee it comes back available. Here's what I'd suggest: let me send you the proposal documents today so you have everything you need for your conversation. If you want to hold the date while you talk — no financial commitment, just a 72-hour hold — I can do that for you right now. After 72 hours, if you haven't confirmed, I'll release it. Does that work?"

---

### D. "We wanted outdoor only / indoor only"

"Completely understand — and I want to make sure I give you the full picture of how the space works. Our outdoor ceremony garden is the primary ceremony space; the reception hall and covered patio are separate areas that most couples don't see as 'backup' — they're part of the event flow. For the ceremony itself: Henderson averages 294 sunny days a year, and we have a weather contingency plan that I walk every couple through before they sign, so you know exactly what happens if the forecast shifts. For couples who want an exclusively indoor ceremony, our reception hall can be converted for that as well. Would it help to do a second walkthrough specifically focused on the flow you're envisioning?"

---

### E. "It's farther than we wanted"

"I hear that — and I want to be honest: Henderson is about 20 minutes from the Strip, and for some guests, that's a consideration. Here's what most of our couples find: because we're single-booking, destination guests actually prefer the drive. There's parking directly on-site for 200+ vehicles, we have two hotel partners within 10 minutes who offer our couples a room block discount, and we can share the contact for a shuttle service that covers the Strip-to-Henderson run starting at $[RATE]. A lot of couples end up treating it as part of the experience — the drive out of the city and into the desert is actually the moment guests start to feel like they've arrived somewhere special. Want me to send you the hotel partner information?"
