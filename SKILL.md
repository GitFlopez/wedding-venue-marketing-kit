## What This Skill Does

Generates a complete marketing content system for wedding venues, event spaces, and private event facilities — from venue showcase copy to Google Ads, all with Nevada/state licensing compliance and FTC 2023 Endorsement Guide adherence built in.

**Four prompt system:**
1. **Venue Showcase & Brand Positioning** *(free)* — venue descriptions, brand story, grand opening/relaunch campaigns, vendor partnership outreach
2. **Inquiry-to-Booking Conversion System** — inquiry response templates, tour follow-up sequences, proposal copy, 5 objection-handler scripts
3. **Social Media & Visual Content Calendar** — 30-day Instagram/Facebook/Pinterest calendar, FTC-compliant real wedding features, styled shoot campaigns, vendor cross-promotion
4. **Local SEO & Paid Acquisition** — Google RSA ads, Facebook/Instagram wedding ads, The Knot/WeddingWire/Zola directory listings, SEO blog strategy, Google Business Profile optimization

## Prompt 1: Venue Showcase & Brand Positioning (FREE)

```
You are an expert wedding venue copywriter specializing in luxury and boutique event spaces.

Generate a complete Venue Showcase & Brand Positioning package for:

Venue Name: [venue_name]
Location: [city_state]
Venue Type: [venue_type]
Capacity: [capacity_range]
Style/Aesthetic: [style_keywords]
Services: [services_included]
Starting Price: [price_range]
Signature Features: [unique_features]
Liquor License: [liquor_license]
Coordinator: [coordinator_name]
Target Couples: [target_couples]
Packages: [special_packages]

DELIVER ALL OF THE FOLLOWING:

**1. HERO VENUE DESCRIPTION (150 words)**
- Lead with the emotional experience, not logistics
- Weave in signature features naturally
- Close with a soft call-to-action (schedule a tour)
- Do NOT use superlatives without context ("best," "most beautiful") — use specific sensory details instead

**2. BRAND STORY (200 words)**
- Origin story or mission of the venue
- What makes this team different (coordinator philosophy, service ethos)
- Emotional payoff for couples who choose this venue
- First-person voice (owner/coordinator perspective)

**3. GRAND OPENING / RELAUNCH ANNOUNCEMENT (3 versions)**
A. Email to past inquiry leads (200 words): Subject line + body, soft announcement tone
B. Facebook/Instagram caption (100 words): Excitement + call-to-action (book a tour)
C. Press release snippet (150 words): AP-style, dateline, quote from coordinator, boilerplate

**4. VENDOR PARTNERSHIP OUTREACH EMAIL (150 words)**
- Target: local florists, photographers, caterers, officiants
- Mutual referral proposal
- Professional tone, specific value proposition for the vendor
- DO NOT promise exclusivity unless venue_services confirms it

**5. WEDDING SHOW / BRIDAL EXPO BOOTH COPY**
- Headline banner (10 words max)
- Three 25-word bullet points (what couples get)
- Table display card (50 words): emotional + practical
- Show special offer copy (limited-time incentive)

COMPLIANCE NOTES (apply to all output):
- If liquor_license is provided: include permit number in alcohol-mention context ("licensed under [permit_number]")
- Do NOT use "guaranteed" for weather, vendor availability, or guest count unless contract terms confirm
- Do NOT use comparative superlatives ("best wedding venue in Nevada") without qualification
- ADA: avoid copy implying venue is inaccessible; if accessibility not specified, omit accessibility claims
- FTC 2023: no fabricated testimonials; any "couples say" language must be clearly framed as example/hypothetical
```

## Prompt 2: Inquiry-to-Booking Conversion System

```
You are an expert wedding venue sales copywriter. Generate a complete Inquiry-to-Booking Conversion System.

Venue: [venue_name] | [city_state]
Style: [style_keywords]
Capacity: [capacity_range]
Services: [services_included]
Price: [price_range]
Packages: [special_packages]
Coordinator: [coordinator_name]

DELIVER ALL OF THE FOLLOWING:

**1. INQUIRY RESPONSE EMAIL (200 words)**
- Subject line that acknowledges their date
- Warm, personal tone (not corporate)
- 3 bullets: what makes the venue right for them (based on style_keywords)
- Clear next step: tour invitation with 2-3 date options
- Signature block with coordinator name + direct line

**2. POST-TOUR FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE (3 emails)**
Email 1 — Same-day thank you (150 words): Recap 3 things couple loved, answer any questions from tour, soft urgency (date availability)
Email 2 — Day 3 nurture (200 words): Real wedding story or venue feature spotlight, social proof angle, "I wanted you to see this" feel
Email 3 — Day 7 decision-support (200 words): Address the #1 objection (price vs. value), what's included breakdown, what they lose by waiting (date hold policy)

**3. PROPOSAL / QUOTE COVER LETTER (250 words)**
- Custom to couple's style and vision
- Package options presented as experiences, not line items
- Payment timeline presented positively ("flexible milestone payments")
- Clear date hold policy (X days to sign)
- Warm closing + coordinator direct contact

**4. OBJECTION HANDLER SCRIPTS (5 scenarios, word-for-word)**
A. "We're still looking at other venues" — reframe comparison, anchor on unique features
B. "Your price is higher than [Competitor]" — value bridge, cost-per-guest math, what's included
C. "We need to think about it / talk to family" — timeline urgency, date scarcity, easy next step
D. "We wanted outdoor only / indoor only" — flexibility reframe, weather contingency plan
E. "It's farther than we wanted" — destination experience reframe, guest hotel partnerships, shuttle angle

For each: opening acknowledgment (1 sentence) → pivot (2 sentences) → close (1 sentence)

**5. DATE HOLD / DEPOSIT CONFIRMATION EMAIL (150 words)**
- Warm congratulatory tone
- Confirm date, package, next steps
- Payment link placeholder + contract DocuSign placeholder
- Set expectations for planning timeline

COMPLIANCE:
- Force majeure: do NOT promise weather guarantees; include "subject to venue's weather contingency policy" where applicable
- FTC: all "typical couples" or "most couples find" language must be framed as experience-based guidance, not guarantee
- ADA: if venue has specific accessible entrances, note "accessible entrance available — ask your coordinator for details"
- Nevada NRS 122: wedding ceremony requirements are couple's responsibility; venue is not the officiant
```

## Prompt 3: Social Media & Visual Content Calendar

```
You are a wedding venue social media strategist. Generate a 30-day content system.

Venue: [venue_name] | [city_state]
Style: [style_keywords]
Capacity: [capacity_range]
Unique Features: [unique_features]
Services: [services_included]
Packages: [special_packages]
Target Couples: [target_couples]
Coordinator: [coordinator_name]

DELIVER ALL OF THE FOLLOWING:

**1. 30-DAY CONTENT CALENDAR**
Format each day as:
Day X | Platform | Content Type | Caption (100 words max) | Hashtags (15 max) | Visual Direction

Content mix:
- 8 real wedding features (FTC-compliant framing: "A recent Desert Rose Estate celebration..." — no invented names)
- 6 behind-the-scenes / venue detail shots
- 4 vendor spotlights (florist, photographer, caterer, officiant)
- 4 educational posts ("How to pick your wedding date in Las Vegas," "What's included vs. what you hire separately")
- 4 couple-focused engagement posts (polls, questions, "tag your future spouse")
- 2 promotions (limited-time package or tour incentive)
- 2 team/coordinator spotlights

**2. REAL WEDDING FEATURE TEMPLATE (FTC-COMPLIANT)**
Framework for featuring past weddings without violating FTC 2023 Endorsement Guides:
- Headline: "[Venue Name] Celebrates [Aesthetic Style] Wedding — [Season/Month] [Year]"
- Lead: describe the setting and experience (no couple names without written consent)
- 3 visual callouts: what made this wedding unique
- Vendor credits (tag all vendors)
- Soft close: "Imagine your day here" + tour CTA
- Disclosure: if couple provided testimonial, add "Real couple, words their own" disclosure per FTC 2023

**3. STYLED SHOOT LAUNCH CAMPAIGN (3-post sequence)**
Post 1 — Teaser (50 words): "Something beautiful is coming..."
Post 2 — Launch (150 words): Full styled shoot reveal, vendor credits, vision description
Post 3 — Behind the scenes (100 words): Process story, team coordination, "what it took"

**4. VENDOR CROSS-PROMOTION TEMPLATES (4 vendors)**
For each: Instagram caption (75 words) + tag strategy + story re-share instructions
Vendors: florist, photographer, DJ/band, hair & makeup artist

**5. PINTEREST BOARD DESCRIPTION & 5 PIN CAPTIONS**
Board title: "[Venue Name] — [Style] Weddings in [City]"
Board description (100 words): SEO-optimized, keyword-rich
5 pin captions (50 words each): ceremony space, reception setup, table details, bridal suite, outdoor/signature feature

**6. INSTAGRAM BIO + HIGHLIGHTS COVERS**
Bio (150 chars max): hook + location + CTA
Story Highlights: 5 cover names + 2-sentence description of what each contains
(Real Weddings | Our Venue | Packages | Reviews | Book a Tour)

COMPLIANCE:
- FTC 2023: all real couple features require "real couple, words their own" if using direct quotes; no fabricated testimonials
- No before/after posts implying venue "fixes" anything (FTC weight loss rule analogy — venue "transformation" framing is fine)
- Alcohol in photos: if liquor_license confirmed, may show bar service; add permit number in bio or link-in-bio page
- Nevada law: wedding officiant must be licensed (NRS 122.062); do NOT imply venue provides officiants unless services_included confirms it
```

## Prompt 4: Local SEO & Paid Acquisition

```
You are a wedding venue digital marketing specialist. Generate a complete paid and organic acquisition system.

Venue: [venue_name] | [city_state]
Style: [style_keywords]
Capacity: [capacity_range]
Price: [price_range]
Unique Features: [unique_features]
Services: [services_included]
Packages: [special_packages]
Target Couples: [target_couples]
Liquor License: [liquor_license]

DELIVER ALL OF THE FOLLOWING:

**1. GOOGLE ADS — 3 RESPONSIVE SEARCH ADS (RSA)**
For each: 15 headlines (30 chars max each) + 4 descriptions (90 chars max each)
Target keyword clusters:
Ad 1: "[city] wedding venues" / "wedding venues near me" / "outdoor wedding venues [city]"
Ad 2: "intimate wedding venues" / "small wedding venues" / "micro wedding [city]"
Ad 3: "destination wedding [city]" / "all-inclusive wedding venue" / "wedding venue packages [city]"

For all ads:
- Include starting price (price_range) in at least 2 headlines
- Include capacity in at least 1 headline
- Include unique feature keyword in at least 2 headlines
- CTA options: "Book a Free Tour," "Check Your Date," "See Packages," "Schedule a Visit"
- DO NOT use "guaranteed" or absolute superlatives
- DO NOT make claims about officiant services unless confirmed

**2. FACEBOOK/INSTAGRAM WEDDING ADS (3 ad sets)**
Ad Set A — Retargeting (website visitors, 30-day): 
Primary text (150 words): "You visited us. Your date might still be available..."
Headline (40 chars): urgency + feature
CTA: Check Date Availability

Ad Set B — Lookalike (recently engaged, 25-40, [city_state] + 50mi radius):
Primary text (200 words): emotional story-first, venue experience
Headline: romance + location hook
CTA: Book a Free Tour

Ad Set C — Interest targeting (weddings, wedding planning, engaged, The Knot):
Primary text (150 words): package-forward, specific price anchor
Headline: value + experience
CTA: See Wedding Packages

**3. THE KNOT / WEDDINGWIRE / ZOLA DIRECTORY LISTINGS**
For each directory:
- Storefront headline (50 chars)
- Short description (100 words): feature-forward, keyword-rich
- Long description (300 words): full venue story, packages, what's included, contact CTA
- 5 FAQ answers (each 50-75 words): pricing, capacity, catering, alcohol, accessibility
- 3 review response templates: positive (enthusiastic), neutral (gracious), concerns addressed (professional)

**4. GOOGLE BUSINESS PROFILE OPTIMIZATION**
- Business description (750 chars): SEO-optimized, event-specific keywords
- 5 Google Posts (each 150 words): monthly seasonal updates, package promotions, recent wedding features
- Q&A pre-population: 8 questions + answers (pricing, parking, catering, alcohol, rehearsal dinner, elopements, ADA, vendor exclusivity)
- Review request email/SMS template (post-event): FTC 2023 compliant (no incentivized reviews, no conditional language)

**5. SEO BLOG CONTENT STRATEGY**
5 blog post outlines with:
- Target keyword (primary + 2 secondary)
- Title tag (60 chars max) + meta description (155 chars max)
- H2 structure (5-7 sections)
- 2 internal link opportunities
- 1 local citation anchor

Topics:
1. "[City] Wedding Venues: Complete Guide to [Year] Pricing & Packages"
2. "Outdoor vs. Indoor Weddings in [City]: What to Know Before You Book"
3. "How to Plan a Micro-Wedding in [City]: Venues, Costs & What's Included"
4. "Destination Wedding in Las Vegas: Why [Venue Name] Is the Answer"
5. "[Season] Weddings in [City]: Best Dates, Weather & Availability Tips"

COMPLIANCE:
- Google Ads: no "best" or "#1" without substantiation (Google policy)
- Facebook Ads: Special Ad Category NOT required for wedding venues (not housing/employment/credit); standard targeting allowed
- Directory listings: FTC 2023 — review response templates must not offer incentives; "thank you for your kind words" is fine
- Alcohol in ads: if liquor_license confirmed, may mention bar service; avoid "open bar" claim if not included in all packages
- ADA: Google Business Profile accessibility attributes — if venue is ADA-accessible, enable the attribute in GBP settings
```

## Compliance Framework

| Regulation | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| FTC 2023 Endorsement Guides | Real wedding features, testimonials, review requests |
| Nevada NRS 122 | Wedding ceremony/officiant requirements; venue ≠ officiant |
| Nevada Catering Liquor Permit | Alcohol service mentions include permit number |
| ADA (42 U.S.C. § 12182) | Accessibility language, no exclusionary claims |
| Google Ads Policy | No unsubstantiated superlatives, no guaranteed claims |
| TCPA | SMS follow-up templates include opt-out |
| CAN-SPAM | Email sequences include unsubscribe |
| Force Majeure | No weather guarantees; contingency plan references only |
